The course promise

What you’ll leave with

By the end of two days, you’ll be better prepared to make informed decisions, lead with confidence, and ensure AI delivers value in a way that aligns with organisational values and societal expectations.

A decision discipline

Frameworks to make clear, defensible decisions about when and how AI should be used.

A leadership stance

A position on responsible AI you can communicate and stand behind under pressure.

A practical toolkit

Guardrails, oversight, incident response and change leadership you can apply on Monday.

The airspace metaphor

Own the airspace

An air traffic controller doesn’t keep airspace safe by stopping all movement. Safety comes from visible rules, separation standards, and clear coordination points that let movement happen responsibly.

Leading AI is similar. You’re not expected to operate every tool or approve every decision personally. Your job is to own the conditions under which your team thinks, decides, discloses and escalates. That’s your airspace — and it’s what this course helps you lead.

One reassurance before we start. You won’t be put on the spot for not “knowing AI.” Throughout, we use plain language — “AI-assisted,” “synthesised,” “system-supported” — and focus on leadership judgement, not technical skill.
