Defining AI Leadership Direction

Policy tells people what the organisation permits. A leadership position tells people what you expect, what needs caution, and what you’ll stand behind when AI is involved. If you don’t make it explicit, your team will infer one anyway — from your behaviour and your silence.

Policies cover your organisation. Your position covers your airspace.

Module overview

Policy is not the same as direction

Most teams don’t work from policy alone. They work from what’s made visible in practice: what the leader notices, what gets questioned, what gets praised, what’s allowed to pass — and what happens when pressure rises.

If your position is unclear, your team will still form one. It will be inferred from your behaviour, your silence, and what seems to be tolerated. This module makes that position explicit — and tests whether it holds when convenience would otherwise take over.

A working draft, not a doctrine. You’re not aiming for perfect wording today. You’re building a first-draft leadership position clear enough to use, test and strengthen over time.

What is your silence currently permitting?

Silence is rarely experienced as neutrality. In the absence of an explicit position, your team still receives a message — from what you reward and what you let pass. Tick what’s currently true of you, and read the message they’re likely receiving.

The clarity trap. A position is only useful if it reduces ambiguity in practice. The test: would a reasonable team member, working quickly and in good faith, know what to do here without needing to ask? If not, it isn’t clear enough yet.

Leading under pressure — language to hold the line

Positions sound reasonable written quietly in a workbook. They’re tested under executive urgency, deadlines, enthusiasm and FOMO. Pick the pressure most real for you and pre-load language you can actually use — without defaulting to “just roll it out” or “shut it down”.

A quick leadership check

Decision clarity

What decision will this actually shape?

Accountability

Who would explain this if it went wrong?

Risk signal

What’s the most credible downside?

Red-line trigger

Does this cross something I’ve already said no to?

Automation creep

Is this starting to act on our behalf?

What a strong position sounds like

A strong position makes six things clear: what AI is for, what value constrains it, what role AI may play, where the line is, what must stay human, and when to pause or escalate. Compare.

✓ Strong leadership position

“In my area, AI assists with first drafts, summarising and internal analysis, but it must not replace human judgement about what matters. Human judgement must remain visible in review and sign-off. Important context must not be lost as information moves through the workflow. If something feels unclear, incomplete or hard to explain, people are expected to pause or escalate. Any output that reaches a customer, affects a team member’s standing, or informs an external commitment requires a named person to review it and sign off. We do not let AI make final decisions about individuals. When something goes wrong, the question is not what the AI did. It is what we approved.”

✕ Weak leadership position

“We’re supportive of the team using AI to work smarter. Just be sensible about it, check the outputs, and use your judgement. If something looks off, flag it. We’ll review things as we go and adjust if we need to. Obviously follow the policy.”

Why it fails: nothing here tells a team member working at speed what they may do, where the line is, or what must stay human. It’s delegated ambiguity dressed as support.

Final activityPressure-test in pairs

Pressure-test your position

In the room, you’d read your position aloud in 60 seconds and your partner would test it — not rewrite it. Run the same three tests on yourself, then tighten the wording.

1 · Permission

What would a reasonable team member now believe they’re allowed to do?

2 · The line

Where is the line still unclear, if anywhere?

3 · The wobble

Under pressure, what part is most likely to wobble?
The three-question gut check: Would I say this out loud to my team? Would a reasonable team member know what to do from it? Would I still stand behind it if an AI issue surfaced tomorrow? If not — tighten the wording now.

⏎ Take-home only — AI as a thought partner (not in the session)

Once your position is locked by your own judgement, you can paste it into an AI tool and ask it — as a neutral thought partner — to surface ambiguities, unstated assumptions, pressure points, and ways it could be misread as permission. It must not rewrite or improve your position. This reinforces the principle: AI is most useful after judgement is formed, not in place of it.

Key takeaways

Policy ≠ direction. Policy covers the organisation; your position covers your airspace.
Silence is permission. Say nothing and your team infers a stance from your behaviour.
AI serves strategy — or builds a shadow one. It amplifies the strongest signals unless you anchor it.
Values become constraints. They define what you won’t trade away, even when the case sounds strong.
Pressure is the real test. If your line creates no tension, it may be too vague to shape behaviour.
Make six things clear. What AI is for, where the line is, and what stays human — at minimum.
